Background

Supporting ...

Recognising the depth of skills gained through experience.

One of the best ways to learn is by doing.

There is a balance between abstract (academic) learning and authentic (real-world) learning, each feeding of the other to enable learner growth.

Many people do not have their authentic learning (skills gained through experience) recognised.

Recognition of skills gained (skills identity) leads to economic identity.

Economic identity of individuals leads to a better society (collective).

selfdriven helps with:

  • Individuals having their authentic learning (skills gained through experience) recognised.
  • Sharing of the skills in a structured way using Endorsements, Achievements and the selfdriven Universal Skill Set.
  • Traceability of validation - helping with trust between people and organisations that don't know each other.
